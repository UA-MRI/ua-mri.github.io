## Research Interests
I am focused on improving and accelerating abdominal quantitative MRI through pulse sequence development, advanced image reconstruction, and improved parameter estimation. My research focuses on T2 mapping using the radial turbo spin-echo (RADTSE) and radial gradient-echo and spin-echo (RADGRASE) sequences. 

## Background

In 2017, I graduated from the College of the Holy Cross in Worcester, MA with a B.A. in Mathematics. Afterwards, I worked two years as an image analyst for Invicro in Boston, MA. In 2019, I moved to Tucson, AZ to pursue my PhD in Applied Mathematics (Minoring in Statistics and Data Science) at the University of Arizona. I received my M.S. in 2020 and defended my PhD in 2025. I am currently living in Seattle, WA working with the lab remotely.
